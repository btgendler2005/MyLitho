Size & thickness

A flat mat shrunk in from each photo's own edge, on all 4 sides — the panel's own outer size stays exactly Face size (unlike the single-panel Border/mat, which grows the panel), since the frame is sized around Face size. A bigger mat gives cleaner, more even contact into the corner-post grooves and against the cap/floor, at the cost of showing less of each photo.

Image adjustments (shared by all 5 faces)
Frame

Exports 7 files: 5 panels, a base (floor + 4 corner posts), and a top cap. Drop the 4 side panels down into the base's corner grooves and let them rest on the floor; slide the top panel into the cap's rabbet from underneath; press the cap down last to lock everything in place. No glue. A deeper groove holds panels more securely but hides more of each photo's edge; a bigger post size gives more room for that groove.

LED puck

The base's floor is solid with a round recess sized to your puck light, plus a shallow channel out to one edge for its cord. Measure your actual puck and set Puck diameter to match (add a couple mm of slack) and Pocket depth to close to its full thickness so it sits set into the base rather than perched on top — the floor grows to stay solid underneath, independent of Corner post size. Set Pocket depth to 0 for a flat floor with no recess.
